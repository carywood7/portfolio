The Background Advantage

Business Before Code

I spent years inside the workflows software is supposed to improve.

Before building software products, I worked across sales, digital marketing, media buying, funnel development, client delivery and CRM implementation. Much of that work involved US-based clients, performance campaigns and systems that had to produce measurable outcomes.

I learned where leads get lost, why teams stop using tools, how poor permissions create operational risk, and why a technically correct system can still fail when it does not match the way people actually work.

What That Changes

I think about adoption, operations and outcomes from the beginning.

The interface, data model, workflow and business objective are not separate concerns. They are different parts of the same product.

How I Work

01

Understand the Operation

Start with the people, business rules, handoffs, risks and result the system needs to support.

02

Model the System

Define clear entities, account boundaries, permissions, states and workflows before adding interface complexity.

03

Build in Safe Batches

Implement one connected capability at a time with deliberate browser checks, automated tests and clean checkpoints.

04

Improve Through Use

Treat the first working version as the beginning of the feedback loop, not the end of product development.
